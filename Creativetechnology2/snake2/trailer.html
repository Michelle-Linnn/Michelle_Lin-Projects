> HOW TO USE THE RECORD BUTTON

  1. Click RECORD & DOWNLOAD.
  2. The trailer plays once while being captured (30 seconds — please don't switch tabs).
  3. Your browser downloads the finished video automatically.
  4. Rename the file to trailer.mp4 (or highlights.mp4) and drop it next to infinisnake.html.

Most browsers export .webm. If your assignment specifically needs .mp4, convert with ffmpeg: ffmpeg -i trailer.webm trailer.mp4

> SCREEN-RECORD BACKUP

If the download button misbehaves in your browser, you can also screen-record the preview:

  • macOS: Shift+Cmd+5 → record selected portion
  • Windows: Win+G (Game Bar) → record
  • Any OS: OBS Studio (free) · QuickTime · ShareX

Record at 1080p+ if possible. Click PLAY on the trailer, start your screen recorder a beat before, and stop it a beat after.

> FILMING GUIDE — "SEVERAL PEOPLE PLAYING" VIDEO

The assignment wants a separate video of real humans playing with your custom controller. Here is a 6-shot plan that reads well in under 60 seconds. Shoot each shot 2–3 times so you have options in the edit.

01

ESTABLISHING / HANDS ON CONTROLLER

~4s · close-up · no audio needed

Low angle, shallow focus if possible. Camera on the controller. Hands enter frame, grip it, one finger presses a key.

Why: gives the audience a sense of the physical thing before they see the screen.
02

PLAYER #1 — REACTION ON FIRST DEATH

~5s · medium shot · keep their face in frame

Shoot from beside the monitor so you see both their face and the screen in profile. Wait for the "aaaah" moment when their snake eats itself.

Why: one honest reaction sells the whole game better than a trailer ever will.
03

OVER-THE-SHOULDER GAMEPLAY

~6s · shooter behind the player

Frame the monitor over the player's shoulder. Shoot during an active delivery run — crates being eaten, warehouse approach, combo popping.

Why: shows the game in context without becoming a screen capture.
04

TWO-PLAYER HOT-SEAT PASS

~6s · wide shot

Two friends on the couch. Player A dies dramatically, hands the controller to Player B without breaking eye contact with the screen.

Why: implies replayability and "one more try" culture.
05

TALKING-HEAD QUOTE

~4s · medium close-up · sync audio

One playtester looks at the camera and says one short line. Use one of these prompts or pick your own: "I don't know what I just played." · "Why am I emotionally invested in a rectangle?" · "I have to try that again."

Why: free review quote that doubles as a comedy beat.
06

CLOSING GROUP SHOT

~3s · ends the video

All your playtesters crowded around one screen, laughing. Freeze frame. Overlay the game title "INFINISNAKE" in your edit.

Why: humans always make the best end card.

> EDIT ORDER (RECOMMENDED)

  1. 01 Hands on controller (4s)
  2. 03 Over-shoulder gameplay (6s)
  3. 02 First-death reaction (5s)
  4. 04 Hot-seat pass (6s)
  5. 05 Talking head quote (4s)
  6. 06 Group shot + title card (3s)

Total: ~28–32 seconds. Cross-cut with 1–2 seconds of footage from the animated trailer above (between shots 03 and 04, or as B-roll) to connect the two videos stylistically.

Save final edit as trailer.mp4 in the same folder as infinisnake.html.
